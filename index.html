Magnificent Lions began in Tyler Bradford's head long before anybody heard the first note. It was birthed in secret as songs took shape, scratched together on napkins and in corners of notebooks, each one coming to life, it would seem, fully formed and with cohesive vision. He took a chance, booked studio time, and began the arduous process of making it sound "out there" the way it already did in his head.



After some time, Tyler scrapped a band together. David Jaramillo, an old coworker, was the first to come, and soon found himself in the role of lead guitar. His friend, Tyler McDaniel, former drummer for Chattanooga metalcore band Anchored at Sea, came next and settled behind the kit. Hamilton Barber, the guitarist in They Came Running, a band he and Tyler had toured together in for years, came enthusiastically as the bass player. Not one month after the band was assembled, they'd booked their first show and were off.



Sonically, their self-titled debut album is a shot of fresh air across the bow of an increasingly bloated music industry. Written and performed exclusively by Tyler, it delivers a ten-track journey through almost a decade of songwriting. But playing it live was a different beast altogether.



In 2018, a year after recording begain and before it was even finished, MagLi thundered onto the Chattanooga, TN music scene with their aggressive, cryptic, baritone rock sound. They became a staple in the area for any lineup needing an act committed to delivering a tone-heavy, aggressive experience infused with indie rock sentimentality. Magnificent Lions’ self-titled debut album was released on January 1, 2019, and they are currently writing a follow-up EP, which they plan on tracking live and releasing later in 2019.



Live, the songs that had once been the passion project of one person began to take on a new life expressed through energy, emotion, and raw aggression—and demonstrate what new directions the band will be heading in the endeavors to come.
